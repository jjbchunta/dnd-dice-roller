Writing Dice Expressions

When interacting with the home page, you'll see a list of input fields similar to the ones displayed below:

All of these input fields are the slots where you can enter your character's spell's dice expressions for each ability level. And a dice expression is just the math that goes behind a cast.

Let's say you had a spell that takes 2 rolls of a 8 sided dice, and adds your strength modifier to the result of those two rolls. To write out this expression, you'd use the proper notation to represent these different calculations.

The dice rolls for that example would be represented with the expression 2d8, where the first number is the times you roll the dice, the second number is the sides of that dice, and the d seperates the two. Everytime this expression is evaluated, the result is random.

Furthermore, once you define your stat sheet, your ability modifiers can be included by using the slug for that ability. The bonuses being automatically calculated.

  • Strength = str
  • Dexterity = dex
  • Constitution = con
  • Intelligence = int
  • Wisdom = wis
  • Charisma = cha
  • Proficiency = prof
